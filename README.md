# Digital-Literacy-Project : Digital literacy programs for older adults

Objective: To empower older adults (typically 55+) with essential digital skills, enabling them to stay connected, access online services, and navigate technology confidently and safely. 


Program Examples:

Great Light Healing Community Services (Canada): Offers a 16-week in-person program covering computer basics, email, web browsing, and online scam prevention. Includes hands-on training and one-on-one support.

AARP Foundation & OATS (USA): Runs Senior Planet, offering free online and in-person classes on Zoom, email, social media, and internet safety.  Classes available in English, Spanish, and Chinese.

AT&T Connected Learning: Provides free self-paced online modules and in-person support at Connected Learning Centers, focusing on digital basics and online safety.

Cyber-Seniors: A nonprofit that pairs seniors with youth mentors for personalized tech tutoring via video calls, promoting intergenerational learning.

NIIT Foundation (India): Delivers tailored training in basic computing, internet use, and digital tools in a supportive environment for seniors.


Curriculum Focus:

Basic device operation (smartphones, tablets, computers)

Internet navigation and search skills

Email and messaging apps (WhatsApp, Facebook Messenger)

Video calling (Zoom, FaceTime)

Online safety: recognizing scams, phishing, and protecting personal data

Accessing e-services (telehealth, online banking, government portals) 


Delivery Methods: In-person workshops, virtual classes, on-demand videos, and one-on-one tutoring to accommodate different learning preferences and accessibility needs. 


Impact: Reduces social isolation, improves access to healthcare and services, enhances independence, and builds confidence in using technology
